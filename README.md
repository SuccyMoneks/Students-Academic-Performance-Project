# Students’ Academic Performance Analysis – Power BI Project

## Project Objective

The main objective of this project is to use Power BI to analyze students' academic performance and turn unprocessed educational data into engaging visualizations. In order to help educators and stakeholders better understand student performance, test-taking participation, overall academic achievement and areas for improvement, the objective is to compare student results, identify patterns, and convey important insights.

## Project Process

  1.	Data Understanding
     
In order to determine the variables that were available and the kinds of insights that could be produced, the first step was to examine and comprehend the dataset.
The dataset includes data on students' academic achievement as well as background characteristics including gender, parental level of education, lunch type, test preparation course participation, scores in subjects like math, reading, and writing. Understanding these variables helped determine how different factors might influence students’ academic outcomes.

  2.	Data Preparation and Cleaning
     
Before creating the dashboard, the dataset was prepared to ensure it was suitable for analysis.
The steps included:
  - Reviewing data types for each column
  - Checking for missing or inconsistent values via Excel
  -	Ensuring subject scores were correctly formatted as numeric values
  -	Importing the dataset into Power BI
  -	Creating calculated measures such as total student count and average scores.

This step ensured that the data was clean, structured, and ready for visualization.

  3.	Data Modeling
     
Basic data modeling was applied to support analysis and calculations. Measures and aggregations were created to calculate:
  -	Total number of students
  -	Average scores across subjects
  -	Distribution of students based on test preparation course completion

These calculations made it easier to generate meaningful visuals in the dashboard.

  4.	Data Visualization

To display the data in an understandable and engaging manner, several visuals were created using Power BI. Among them were:
  -	The percentage of students who finished the exam preparation course compared to those who did not is displayed in a donut chart.
  -	Bar charts for comparing subject-specific performance
  -	To Identify the subject performance between genders is displayed in the column chart
  -	Pie charts that shows the distribution of students by lunch type, etc.

Finding patterns and trends in the dataset was made easier by these representations.

  5.	Key Insights from the Dashboard

The visual analysis revealed several important insights about student performance:

  -	Test preparation influences performance
  -	Majority of students did not complete the preparation course
  -	Performance differences across subjects
  -	Variations in student performance
  -	Impact of Parental Level of Education on Student Performance
  -	Influence of Race/Ethnicity on Average Subject Scores

## Key Insights

  -	Students who completed the test preparation course tend to perform better across subjects compared to those who did not complete it. This suggests that preparatory programs can positively impact academic outcomes.
  -	A larger portion of students did not take the preparation program. This indicates a potential opportunity for institutions to encourage more participation in such programs.
  -	Some subjects show higher average scores than others, indicating areas where students perform stronger and areas that may require additional academic support.
  -	The dataset shows that student outcomes vary based on background factors, highlighting that academic success can be influenced by multiple educational and social factors.
  -	The analysis shows a noticeable relationship between parents’ educational attainment and students’ academic performance. Students whose parents have higher levels of education (bachelor’s degree or master’s degree) tend to record higher average scores across math, reading, and writing. Students whose parents have some college or an associate degree also perform relatively well, although slightly lower than those with parents holding advanced degrees. Students whose parents have high school education or lower generally record lower average scores across the subjects.
  -	The dashboard analysis comparing race/ethnicity groups with subject averages reveals variations in academic performance across groups. Some race/ethnicity groups consistently record higher average scores across reading and writing, indicating stronger performance in language related subjects. Certain groups demonstrate more balanced performance across math, reading, and writing, suggesting more consistent academic outcomes. Other groups show lower average scores in specific subjects, particularly in math, indicating potential gaps in subject specific performance.

## Conclusion

The analysis of students’ academic performance reveals that factors such as test preparation, parental level of education, and race/ethnicity show noticeable relationships with students’ scores across math, reading, and writing. Students who completed test preparation programs and those with parents who have higher educational attainment generally perform better. These insights highlight the importance of academic support programs, parental involvement, and equitable access to learning resources to help improve overall student performance.
